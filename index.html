Cryptocurrencies are digital currencies which are decentralized, meaning that an individual body, like a government, does not back them up.

Like other currencies, their value is derived from an agreed upon idea of what they're worth, rather than anything intrinsic.

Since they are decentralized, one main authority does not validate transactions. Rather, a collaborative computer network validates actions together. This is called the blockchain.

In short, computers work together to solve complex equations in order to process transactions. This means it's hard to take down, but also very energy-intensive.
